WELCOME
“True north for those who build wealth together.”
This document is the steward of our visual and verbal identity. It captures who The Meridian Collective
is, how we present ourselves to the world, and the standards every partner, vendor, and member
should follow when representing our brand.
The Meridian Collective is more than a real estate investment company. We are a coalition of
disciplined, like-minded investors charting a deliberate course toward generational wealth. Every
meridian on a globe is a line of guidance — a fixed reference that orients travelers wherever they stand.
Our brand is built on that same promise: clarity, direction, and shared purpose.
Use these guidelines whenever you create something that carries our name — from a property flyer to a
closing document, from a social post to a contractor agreement. Consistency is how a young brand earns
trust quickly. Treat this book as the source of truth.

PERSONALITY & VOICE
IF THE MERIDIAN COLLECTIVE WERE A PERSON
She is the friend with the spreadsheet. She has read the operating agreement twice. She speaks plainly,
asks the harder question, and never raises her voice. She is warm in the room and rigorous on the page.

PERSONALITY ATTRIBUTES
WE ARE

Confident, considered, warm, exact, principled.

WE ARE NOT

Loud, salesy, jargon-heavy, performative, exclusive.

WE SOUND LIKE

A trusted advisor briefing you over coffee — informed, calm, generous
with context.

WE NEVER SOUND
LIKE

An infomercial, a hype reel, or a guru’s landing page.

VOICE PRINCIPLES

♦Plain over polished. Say it the way you would say it to a sharp friend who has never bought a
house.
♦Specific over sweeping. Replace “significant returns” with “a 14% projected cash-on-cash return.”
♦Calm over urgent. We do not manufacture scarcity. Good deals get done because the work was
done.
♦We over I. Ownership and credit belong to the collective.
♦Receipts over rhetoric. Every claim is paired with a number, a source, or a story.

CLEAR SPACE
Always preserve a margin of clear space around the logo equal to the height of the capital “M” in
MERIDIAN. No text, image, edge, or graphic element should ever intrude on this protected zone.

MINIMUM SIZE
DIGITAL

Minimum 200 pixels wide for the full crest + wordmark.

PRINT

Minimum 1.5 inches wide. For anything smaller, use the wordmark
only.

FAVICON / AVATAR

Use the isolated globe + star crest, cropped square.

COLOR APPLICATION RATIO
Use color in proportion. Lead with neutrals. Let gold be the punctuation, not the paragraph.

♦60% · Pearl White & Champagne Mist. The canvas. Most surface area should breathe.
♦25% · Midnight Walnut & Soft Charcoal. Type, structure, and weight.
♦10% · Heritage Bronze. Headings, dividers, and emphasis.
♦5% · Imperial Gold & Burnished Amber. Hero accents — the moment that makes it ours.

ACCESSIBILITY
Body copy should always meet WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1) against its background. Midnight Walnut on
Pearl White is the safest pairing. Imperial Gold should never be used for long-form text — it is a brand
accent, not a reading color.

IMAGERY & PHOTOGRAPHY
Imagery is the second voice of the brand. Choose photography that feels honest, warm, and
architectural — never staged, never stock-generic.

PHOTOGRAPHY PRINCIPLES

♦Natural light. Golden hour, soft shadows, warm undertones. Avoid harsh flash.
♦Real properties. Show the homes we actually own, work on, or aspire to. No royalty-free
Mediterranean villas.
♦People as humans, not models. When members or partners appear, they look like themselves —
considered, dressed for the room, unposed.
♦Atlanta in the frame. Where context helps, let the city show — brick bungalows, oak canopies, the
skyline at dusk.
♦Documentary, not aspirational. Before/after construction shots, walkthroughs, signing days. The
work is the story.

SUBJECT MATTER — YES

♦Single-family homes mid-renovation, with materials and craftsmanship visible.
♦Architectural details: trim, hardwood, tile, brick, light through windows.
♦Member portraits in professional but warm settings.
♦Maps, plans, and documents shot from above on warm wood or stone.
♦The Atlanta metro skyline and recognizable neighborhood landmarks.

SUBJECT MATTER — NO

♦Stock images of handshakes, glass skyscrapers, or gold bars.
♦Luxury cars, private jets, or other wealth-display tropes.
♦Generic “diverse team in conference room” imagery.
♦Heavy filters, oversaturated HDR, or cool blue color grading.

IMAGE TREATMENT
When color-grading, lean toward warm shadows and a slight golden cast in highlights. Avoid cool,
teal-and-orange cinematic grading. Black-and-white is acceptable for portraiture and archival use.

SOCIAL MEDIA

♦Profile image: Isolated globe + compass crest, cropped square on Pearl White.
♦Cover image: Wordmark on Midnight Walnut with a subtle gold flourish, sized to platform spec.
♦Post template: Pearl White background, Cambria headline in Heritage Bronze, body in Calibri Soft
Charcoal, single Imperial Gold accent.
♦Hashtags: Use sparingly. #MeridianCollective for owned content. Avoid generic real estate
hashtag clusters.

PROPERTY MARKETING

♦Yard signs & flyers: Logo top-center, property photo dominant, key terms in Calibri Bold. Never
overlay text on the logo.
♦Listing description: Lead with three sentences in our voice. No exclamation points. No “WOW!” or
“Must see!”
♦Walkthrough videos: Open with the gold compass star, hold for 1 second, then live footage.
Lower-thirds in Cambria, Heritage Bronze.

BRAND STEWARDSHIP
A brand is a promise repeated. These standards only work if every member, partner, and vendor follows
them. When in doubt, choose the more restrained option — less is almost always more on this brand.

APPROVAL PROCESS

♦Tier 1 · Internal use. Member emails, internal documents, group communications — no approval
needed if these guidelines are followed.
♦Tier 2 · External use. Anything that leaves the collective — vendor agreements, marketing
materials, social posts — reviewed by the designated brand steward before publication.
♦Tier 3 · Co-branded or paid media. Any use of the logo with another organization’s mark, or any
paid advertising, requires unanimous member approval and review by counsel.

ASSET LIBRARY
All approved logo files, color swatches, fonts, and templates are housed in the collective’s shared drive.
Always pull from the master files. Never recreate, never screenshot.
LOGO FILES

PNG, SVG, and EPS in primary, white-background, dark-background,
and crest-only versions.

COLOR SWATCHES

Adobe ASE, Procreate palette, and screenshot reference card.

TEMPLATES

Letterhead, invoice, member memo, social post, and yard sign
templates.

THIS DOCUMENT

PDF and Word versions, version-controlled and dated.

LIVING DOCUMENT
These guidelines will evolve as the collective grows. Significant changes — new colors, expanded logo
system, sub-brands — require member discussion and a new edition of this book. Minor refinements
may be issued as addenda.
