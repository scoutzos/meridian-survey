Overview

All 6 members responded: Courtney Mosely, Odessa Patterson, Raquel Twine, Tiffany Stallworth, Aaliyah Thomas, Peggee

25 categories, 40+ questions covering every major operating agreement topic.

Overall: Strong foundation of shared values with a handful of critical items that need group discussion before the OA can be drafted.

Where We Agree

  • Risk tolerance: Moderate — calculated risks with proper research (5 of 6; Peggee conservative)
  • Outside deals: Allowed with full disclosure, no restrictions
  • Dispute resolution: Mediation first, then arbitration — no litigation
  • IP/Brand: LLC owns everything; departing members have no claim
  • Insurance: Full coverage — let a broker recommend based on activity
  • Accountability: Everyone wants to be held accountable
  • Exit during active deals: Cannot exit while deals are in progress
  • Profit structure: Deal-by-deal waterfall based on contribution to each deal
  • Distribution timing: After each deal closes
  • Financing: Hard money for flips, conventional for holds — leverage preferred
  • Involuntary removal: Fraud, failure after written notice, felony
  • Dissolution: Unanimous vote required; complete active projects first
  • Non-compete: No restrictions after exit (non-solicitation recommended)
  • Fiduciary duties: Duty of care yes; duty of loyalty modified to allow outside deals
  • Conflict style: Direct but respectful, with mutual accountability
  • Financial transparency: Share what is relevant to LLC decisions; personal finances stay private

Where We Differ — Must Resolve Before OA

1. Ownership Structure — Biggest Open Question

  • Courtney: Capital + sweat equity combination
  • Raquel: Equal ownership for all
  • Odessa: Multiple views (equal, per-deal, weighted by capital)
  • Tiffany/Aaliyah: Weighted by contribution or per-deal
  • Peggee: Per-deal basis
Recommendation: Hybrid — equal base membership, but deal-by-deal profit splits based on documented contribution to each deal.

2. Initial Capital

  • Range: $5,000 fixed (Odessa, Peggee) to variable based on capacity (Courtney, Raquel, Aaliyah)
Recommendation: $5,000 minimum within 30 days, with option for additional contributions earning preferred return.

3. Personal Guarantees on Loans — Major Split

  • Courtney, Raquel, Odessa: All members guarantee equally
  • Tiffany, Peggee: No personal guarantees — non-recourse only
  • This affects available financing. Most hard money lenders require personal guarantees.
Recommendation: Guarantees voluntary. Members who guarantee receive a 2-5% premium on that deal's profits.

4. Managing Member Structure

  • No clear consensus: all members (Courtney), two co-managers (Odessa, Raquel, Aaliyah), rotating (Tiffany, Peggee)
Recommendation: Two co-managing members for Year 1, reassess annually.

5. Tax Distributions

  • Most want the LLC to distribute for taxes before K-1s
  • Raquel: Members handle their own taxes
Recommendation: Mandatory annual tax distribution. Without it, members owe taxes on income they never received in cash.

6. Spousal Consent

  • Tiffany/Aaliyah: Required before signing OA
  • Odessa/Raquel: Recommended but not required
  • Peggee: Unnecessary if divorce provisions are strong
  • Courtney: Unsure
Recommendation: Required. Cheap insurance that prevents future disputes in Georgia courts.

7. Maximum Personal Risk (Year 1)

  • Peggee: $5K | Courtney/Raquel: $10K | Odessa/Tiffany: $5-10K | Aaliyah: $50K
Recommendation: Structure deals based on the lowest comfort level. Scale up as trust and profits grow.
